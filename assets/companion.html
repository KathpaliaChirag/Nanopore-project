1 · Watch O(T) and O(T²) diverge

Drag the sequence length. The RNN does less total arithmetic, but its depth (steps that must wait) explodes while attention's stays pinned at 1. The chip runs depth, not work.

At a nanopore-sized T ≈ 150,000, attention's work bar is off the planet — but its depth bar is still a sliver. That sliver is why parallel hardware loves it.

3 · Flip softmax on and off

The whole caveat in one switch. Raw L (Mamba): weights can be negative, rows sum to anything. After causal softmax (real attention): every weight positive, every row sums to exactly 1.00. Same numbers underneath.

4 · Chunk size meets the underflow cliff

Bigger chunks = more of the work is one fast matmul, but a longer cumulative product P that can dive under the floating-point floor. Drag and watch the smallest P inside a chunk approach the cliff.

This is why the shipped code works in log-space (add logs, subtract) instead of dividing tiny numbers — and chunks stay short as a second safety net.
